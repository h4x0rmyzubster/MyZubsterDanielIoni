# 💚 Code of Conduct - MyZubster

Welcome to the MyZubster community! We are passionate about building a truly privacy-first and localized skill exchange platform. Our strength comes from our members, and maintaining a respectful, safe, and inclusive environment is paramount to our success.

This code outlines the standards for acceptable behavior within our project spaces (GitHub discussions, issue tracker) and when representing the MyZubster community publicly. By participating in this open-source project, you agree to abide by these guidelines.

---

## 🌟 Our Pledge

We are committed to providing a welcoming and harassment-free experience for every single person. We believe that everyone deserves to contribute their best work without fear of judgment or abuse.

This pledge applies universally, regardless of:
*   Age or disability status
*   Body size or physical appearance
*   Ethnicity, race, religion, or nationality
*   Gender identity and expression
*   Level of experience (from absolute beginner to seasoned expert)
*   Sexual identity or orientation

---

## ✅ Standards for Behavior

### 👍 What is Expected (Positive Behaviors)
We encourage the following positive practices:
*   **Respectful Dialogue:** Treat all contributors with dignity, even when disagreeing on technical approaches. Focus criticism on code and ideas, never on people.
*   **Empathy and Patience:** Assume good faith from others. Remember that everyone is learning!
*   **Constructive Feedback:** When reviewing code or suggesting features, provide clear, actionable feedback accompanied by explanations of *why* the change is needed.
*   **Inclusivity:** Use welcoming language and acknowledge diverse perspectives in our community discussions.

### 🚫 What is Not Allowed (Unacceptable Behaviors)
The following behaviors are strictly prohibited:
*   **Harassment/Abuse:** Personal attacks, slurs, threats, or any form of persistent harassment directed at an individual.
*   **Discriminatory Content:** Jokes, comments, or images based on race, gender, religion, sexual orientation, etc.
*   **Trolling and Spamming:** Posting deliberately inflammatory content or repetitive spam that derails discussions.
*   **Doxxing/Privacy Violation:** Publishing private information (real names, addresses, personal phone numbers) about any community member without their explicit consent. This is especially critical given our privacy-first focus on Monero payments!
*   **Gatekeeping:** Dismissing ideas or contributions simply because they are novel or outside of the status quo.

## 🧑‍💼 Responsibilities & Scope

### Maintainer Responsibility (Our Promise)
The maintainers of MyZubster are responsible for clarifying these standards and upholding them. We reserve the right to remove, edit, or reject any contributions—including code, comments, and issues—that violate this Code of Conduct.

### Applicability Scope
These guidelines apply:
1.  **In Project Spaces:** All discussions, submissions, and interactions within GitHub (issues, pull requests) and project documentation.
2.  **In Public Representation:** When you represent the MyZubster project or community in any public forum, your actions should uphold these standards of respect and privacy.

---

## 🚨 Reporting Violations (Enforcement)

If you see behavior that violates this Code of Conduct, we ask that you address it with us privately and discreetly:

1.  **Do NOT confront the individual publicly.**
2.  Report the incident directly to the project team via **[INBOX_EMAIL_ADDRESS]**.
3.  Please provide as much detail as possible (screenshots, links, dates/times) so that we can thoroughly investigate the situation.

We promise to review all complaints promptly and discreetly. Any user found to be violating this Code of Conduct may face consequences ranging from a public warning to temporary or permanent removal from the project.
